# Introduction
By concatenating matrices, we mean joining them, for example for two matrices $A, B$ :
$$
A = \begin{pmatrix}
a_{11} & \dots & a_{1n} \\
\vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
a_{m1} & \dots & a_{mn} \\
\end{pmatrix}
, \quad
B = \begin{pmatrix}
b_{11} & \dots & b_{1n} \\
\vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
b_{m1} & \dots & b_{mn} \\
\end{pmatrix}
$$

Their horizontal concatenation is:
$$
( A | B ) = \begin{pmatrix}
a_{11} & \dots & a_{1n} & b_{11} & \dots & b_{1n} \\
\vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
a_{m1} & \dots & a_{mn} & b_{m1} & \dots & b_{mn} \\
\end{pmatrix}
$$

and vertical:
$$
\begin{pmatrix}
A \\
B
\end{pmatrix}
= \begin{pmatrix}
a_{11} & \dots & a_{1n} & b_{11} & \dots & b_{1n} \\
\vdots & \ddots & \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
a_{m1} & \dots & a_{mn} & b_{m1} & \dots & b_{mn} \\
\end{pmatrix}
$$
