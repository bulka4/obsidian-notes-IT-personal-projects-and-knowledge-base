# Introduction
A matrix is two-dimensional vector:
$$
A = (a_{ij})_{m \times n} =  \begin{pmatrix}
a_{11} & \ldots & a_{1n} \\
\vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\
a_{m1} & \ldots & a_{mn} \\
\end{pmatrix}
$$
# Notation
- **Element** - We can denote an element from the $i$-th row and $j$-th column of a matrix as:
	- $a_{ij}$ 
	- $(A)_{ij}$ 
	- $A[i, j]$ 
- **Matrix shape and possible values** - We can write $\large A \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ to denote its shape ($m$ rows and $n$ columns) and possible values (real numbers)
- **Single column** - $\large A_{:j}$ - the $j$-th column
- **Single row** - $\large A_{i:}$ - the $i$-th row
