#MathematicalAnalysis #Mathematics 

# Introduction
Let $(\Omega, \mathcal{F})$ and $(S, \mathcal{S})$ be measurable spaces ([[Measurable space|link]]). Then, a function:
$$
f: \Omega \rightarrow S
$$
is called measurable ($\mathcal{F}/\mathcal{S}$ measurable) if:
$$
\forall B \in \mathcal{S},\ f^{-1}(B) \in \mathcal{F}
$$
Sometimes we also write:
$$
\begin{align}
\text{1) } & f: (\Omega, \mathcal{F}) \rightarrow (S, \mathcal{S}) \\

\text{2) } & f: (\Omega, \mathcal{F}, \mu_1) \rightarrow (S, \mathcal{S}, \mu_2) \\
\end{align}
$$
to indicate what are the $\sigma$-algebras ($\mathcal{F}$ and $\mathcal{S}$) with respect to which it is measurable and what are the considered measures ($\mu_1$ and $\mu_2$).
