# Introduction

# Formal definition
Let $(\mathcal{X}_i,\ \mathcal{F}_i,\ \mu_i),\ i = 1, \ldots, n$ be measure spaces ([[Measure space|link]]). We can construct a new measure space called **product measure space**:
$$
(\mathcal{X}_1 \times \ldots \times \mathcal{X}_n,\ \mathcal{F}_1 \otimes \ldots \otimes \mathcal{F}_n,\ \mu_1 \otimes \ldots \otimes \mu_n)
$$
where $\mathcal{F}_1 \otimes \ldots \otimes \mathcal{F}_n$ is a product $\sigma$-algebra ([[Product sigma algebra|link]]).

The **product measure** $\mu_1 \otimes \ldots \otimes \mu_n$ is the unique measure on $\mathcal{F}_1 \otimes \ldots \otimes \mathcal{F}_n$ satisfying:
$$
\mu_1 \otimes \ldots \otimes \mu_n (A_1 \times \ldots \times A_n) = \mu_1(A_1) \ldots \mu_n(A_n)
$$
for any measurable sets $A_i \in \mathcal{F}_i$, where $A_1 \times \ldots \times A_n$ is a Cartesian product ([[Cartesian product|link]]).
