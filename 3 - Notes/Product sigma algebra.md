# Formal definition
Let $(\mathcal{X}_i,\ \mathcal{F}_i),\ i = 1, \ldots, n$ be measurable spaces ([[Measurable space|link]]). 

The product $\sigma$-algebra is defined as a $\sigma$-algebra generated ([[Sigma algebra generated by a collection of sets|link]]) by all possible cartesian products ([[Cartesian product|link]]) of measurable sets ([[Measurable set|link]]) $A_i \in \mathcal{F}_i$:
$$
\mathcal{F}_1 \otimes \ldots \otimes \mathcal{F}_n = \sigma(\{A_1 \times \ldots \times A_n:\ A_i \in \mathcal{F}_i\})
$$
or in short:
$$
\large
\bigotimes_{i=1}^n \mathcal{F}_i = \mathcal{F}_1 \otimes \ldots \otimes \mathcal{F}_n
$$
