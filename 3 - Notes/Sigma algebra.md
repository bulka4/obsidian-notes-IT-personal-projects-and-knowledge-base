#MathematicalAnalysis #Mathematics 

# Introduction
A sigma algebra ($\sigma$-algebra) of /on a given set ([[Set|link]]) $X$ is a collection of subsets of $X$, denoted by $\mathcal{F}$, that satisfies the following properties:
- $X \in \mathcal{F}$ 
- $A \in \mathcal{F} \implies A^c = X \setminus A \in \mathcal{F}$ 
- For any countable collection of sets $A_1, A_2, ... \in \mathcal{F}$: $\bigcup_{i=1}^\infty A_i \in \mathcal{F}$ 
# Properties of sigma algebra
The following properties are implicated by the definition of a $\sigma$-algebra:
- $\emptyset = X^c \in \mathcal{F}$ 
- Since $\bigcap_i A_i = (\bigcup_i A_i^c)^c$, we also have $\bigcap_i A_i \in \mathcal{F}$
