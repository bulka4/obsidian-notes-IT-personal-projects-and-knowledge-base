# Introduction
A vector space over a field $\mathbb{F}$ is a set $V$ containing vectors ([[Vector|link]]) where we define operations:
- Vector addition:
$$
+: V \times V \rightarrow V
$$
- Scalar multiplication:
$$
\cdot : \mathbb{F} \times V \rightarrow V
$$
These operations must satisfy the following properties for any vectors $u, v, w \in V$ and scalars $a, b \in \mathbb{F}$ :
# Vector addition properties
- Associativity (order of adding doesn't matter)
$$
(u + v) + w = u + (v + w)
$$
- Commutativity
$$
u + v = v + u
$$
- Identity element
	There exist a zero vector $0 \in V$ such that:
$$
v + 0 = v
$$
- Inverse element
	For every vector $v \in V$, there exists its inverse $-v \in V$ such that:
$$
v + (-v) = 0
$$
# Scalar multiplication properties
- Compatibility with scalar multiplication
$$
a(bv) = (ab)v
$$
- Identity scalar
$$
1v = v
$$
- Distributivity over vector addition
$$
a(u + v) = au + av
$$
- Distributivity over scalar addition
$$
(a + b)v = av + bv
$$
